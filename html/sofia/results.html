SOFIA Science Data Archive: Search Results

Results of a search of the SOFIA Science Data Archive appear in two different window panes, each with several tabs.

Terminology

The search results appear in two "panes" (like "window panes"). You can grab and drag the division between the two panes to change their relative sizes.

Layout

The left pane of the search results contains lists of observations that met your criteria. The right pane of the search results contains more details of the observations. The right pane changes depending on what row you have selected (clicked on) in the left pane.

The left pane of the search results could have several tabs -- one called "AOR" that is a list of AORs, and then one for each instrument you have selected ("instrument tabs"). The columns are different in each tab. Each line includes basic information about all the observations which fulfill your search critera. Each tab is its own table, which behaves in the same way that tables throughout IRSA tools with this look and feel. See the Tables section for more details, but in summary you can change the width of the columns, sort by columns, filter by column values, etc.

The corresponding data can be downloaded by checking the box at the start of each row (or the box at the top of the column of boxes to select all) and clicking "Prepare download". (See the section on downloads for more information.)

The right pane of the search results contains three tabs that enable exploration of the data returned by your search. Click on a row in the left pane and the contents of the right pane change. The "Details" tab provides details about the observation. The "Data" tab does not show anything when the AOR tab is in the foreground. The "Coverage" tab shows footprints of all the returned observations on an image; you can interact with the image using the visualization tools.

In the coverage tab, you will see an indication of what portions of the sky are covered by the data meeting your search criteria. Click on the 'layers' icon to bring up a window with the list and to change colors or symbols (see visualization tools). If you have done an all-sky query, the symbols used are:

  • EXES - red square marker
  • FIFI-LS - burgundy square marker
  • FLITECAM - green square marker
  • FORCAST - pink square marker
  • FPI+ - small purple dot
  • GREAT - magenta cross marker
  • HAWC+ - small cyan dot
If you have done a search that is not all-sky, you have a smaller region of sky shown in your coverage tab. For portions of the sky comparable to 30 arcmin or larger, you likely have a HiPS image; smaller regions may be shown on a FITS image. (see visualization tools).

When an instrument tab is in the foreground on the left pane, the "Details" and "Data" tabs change accordingly. The "Data" tab has (for non-proprietary and non-GREAT data) the FITS file corresponding to the observation. You can interact with this FITS file using the visualization tools; you can also overlay catalogs on the image. Note that GREAT data may not be in FITS format, and thus you cannot interact with the data, other than simply downloading it. The "Coverage" tab still indicates an indication of what portions of the sky are covered by the data meeting your search criteria. Note that even if an instrument tab is selected, the coverage tab shows all the results retrieved by the search. The coverage tab and the instrument tab are linked in that if you click on a row in the instrument tab, the corresponding symbol in the coverage tab is highlighted, and vice versa.

If you do a multiple target search, so that you can more easily tie results to input, when you get results back from a batch search, the input RA and Dec are columns appended to the end of your results tables.

Filtering Results

Filters can provide a powerful tool for weeding down search results to the observations you want. Near the top of the search results window, there is this icon: Click on this icon in order to start the process of adding filters. A text entry box appears above each of the current catalog columns, with a small version of the filter icon corresponding to that row on the far left. You can type operators and values in these boxes -- hit return after typing or click in another box to implement the filter. After you impose a filter, then the "filters" icon on the top right of the catalog pane has changed to remind you that there has been a filter applied, in this case just one filter: To clear the filters, click on the cancel filters icon (which also appears after you impose filters): .

There is a whole section just on filtering, which has much more information.

Visualization

Once you have data that you wish to investigate further, you can interact with the FITS files corresponding to most observations. See the Visualization section for more on visualization.
